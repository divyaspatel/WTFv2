You are not alone
The part nobody tells you about going first
You've made a decision that a lot of women describe as one of the most empowering — and most overwhelming — of their lives. The paperwork, the acronyms, the injections, the costs: it's a lot to carry at once.

And it's completely normal to feel excited and terrified in the same breath. You're not behind. You're not doing it wrong. You're exactly where thousands of women have been before you — and they figured it out. So will you.

What they wish they'd known
The things nobody puts in the brochure
"Ask about the monitoring schedule before you commit to a clinic. How early? How often? That logistics question matters more than most people realize until they're in it."
"Talk to your insurance before your first appointment — not after. The conversation is less scary than you think, and it changes everything about what you plan for."
"Give yourself permission to tell someone. The secrecy is isolating — and for most women, the people they told were more supportive than they expected."
And more than anything: women said they wished they'd started the process sooner — not because of age alone, but because it takes more time and energy than the brochure suggests.
